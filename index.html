06 — CONTEXT & ANALYSIS

Critical Turning
Points

Structural events and policy breaks that shaped the Korean economy.

From Han River Miracle
to Advanced Technology State

South Korea entered the 2000s having completed its transition from middle-income to high-income status. The export model led by Samsung, Hyundai and LG deepened global integration.

Aside from the 2008 crisis and 2020 pandemic, Korea maintained stable growth, keeping unemployment below 4%.

From the mid-2010s, demographic decline became clear; population growth turned negative — the key constraint on future expansion.

2000
Post-1997 Recovery
IMF programme completed; reserves rebuilt, KRW strengthened.
2002
FIFA World Cup Co-host
Domestic demand surge; semi-final achievement; national morale boost.
2008
Global Financial Crisis
Growth fell to 0.8%; trade balance turned negative; KRW lost ~25% of value.
2011
Record Trade Surplus
External balance surged to $61.7B; semiconductor and auto exports peaked.
2016
Inflation Low + Presidential Crisis
Historic low 0.71%; Samsung Note 7 recall; Park Geun-hye impeached.
2020
Pandemic Shock
GDP −0.7%; rapid recovery: +4.6% in 2021; FDI hit record $25B.
2022
Inflation Spike
5.09% — highest post-2000; energy/food shock; Bank of Korea rate hikes.
2024–25
AI Chip Boom
Samsung & SK Hynix HBM chips fuel AI infrastructure; exports rebound.
